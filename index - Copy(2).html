What is the Orange bellied parrot

And why is such a big deal?

The orange-bellied parrot is quite special for several reasons:
1. Critically Endangered**: It is one of the world's most endangered birds, with a very small wild population. Conservation efforts are crucial to prevent its extinction.
2. Migratory Behavior: Unlike most parrots, the orange-bellied parrot is migratory. It breeds in Tasmania and migrates to the southern coast of mainland Australia during the winter.
3. Distinctive Appearance: This small parrot is known for its vibrant green plumage and a distinctive orange patch on its belly. Males are generally brighter than females.
4. Unique Habitat: It primarily inhabits coastal and sub-coastal areas, preferring saltmarshes, heathlands, and grassy areas. This specific habitat requirement makes it vulnerable to habitat loss.
5. conservation Efforts: Significant efforts are being made to save this species, including captive breeding programs and habitat restoration projects. These efforts are vital to increase the population and genetic diversity.
